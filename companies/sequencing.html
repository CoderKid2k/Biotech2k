Sequencing

The demand for DNA sequencing is expected to more than triple over the next decade. We are in a new world of gene sequencing and the race for an end to end sequencing system has begun. The desire to push down the cost of sequencing will continue to drive demand. There is a ton of growth demand from areas like Genetic Disease, Women's Health and Oncology. The need for testing in Oncology drives a dominant share of testing. Targeted Therapies, Personalized Cancer vaccines and Minimal Residual Disease are driving an explosion of demand for genetic sequencing. The sequencing market is split into short and long reads. There is a lot of room for companies to innovate in both these segments. The ability to collect genomic information on patients will bring about the era of personalized medicine where genetic sequencing informs treatment. Genomic databases can connect researchers with new therapies to patients in need of those therapies. Access to the population level genetic data from these databases can inform researchers where new treatments are needed.
