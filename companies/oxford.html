Oxford Nanopore

Profile

Oxford uses what is called Nanopore technology. This uses a film with tiny pores in it. They pass a current across the film. Then the sequence is run through the pore. As each different nucleotide passes through the pore, it will make a different sound. This allows the software to record those sounds and build a consensus of the sequence being processed. This is long read sequencing technology. My biggest concern for this company is the stock trades on the European exchanges. It is only listed in the US on the over the counter markets. They allow for US investors to own the company, but the OTC market is extremely illiquid.

Science

When it comes to sequencing, nanopore technology tends to have the strength of versatility. They have a selection of machines which allow for different applications based on the user's needs. This is definitely one of their strengths. They have significantly closed the gap on accuracy by improving this technology and the consensus read software.

Valuation

Cash $600 million estimated

They currently have about $200 million in annual sales projected for 2022. The market for sequencing is estimated to double from 2022 to 2026 from $7 billion to $14 billion. With multiple products on the market, I think Oxford could capture at least $1 billion in sales over the long term. At just 10x sales, that would be worth a $5 billion market cap. Their current value would be around $2 billion based on current sales.

All in, that is a $2.35 billion market cap. Based on the 823 million shares outstanding, that comes to $3.15.
