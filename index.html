Welcome

Investing in biotechnology companies can be very hard. There is a very high level of failure in this industry. There are many complicated concepts that make it hard to understand. This can be very intimidating for many investors. This site is designed to be a source of education and information for investors. It is not designed to be financial advice. If you find interest in any of the companies here, you should consult your financial advisor before making any investment decisions. This is a very risky area of investing. It is not for everyone, and there is a high level of risk as many of these companies fail.

Innovation

The first section is about the discovery and the tracking of science in the biotechnology industry that is leading the innovation of the future. We will look at what technologies in the science appear to be leading the way forward. These will be just my opinions and ideas. Your opinions might be different. That is OK. There is a lot of great science going on in this industry. Some of it will appeal more to one investor than another. It is all about personal opinion. The innovation section will be designed to give ideas for investors as a jumping off point. Not all areas of innovation will work out. Sometimes great science still fails. Sometimes it runs into obstacles or just doesn't live up to expectations. The innovation section will change as science evolves. There will always be science that matures while new science is discovered. This section will always be about "what's next"?

The Companies

The next section of the site will be about all the companies behind the areas of innovation that are driving biotechnology forward. These are the companies that could be the next big breakthrough. Many will fail. The failure rate in biotechnology is over 90%. That means the vast majority of these companies can and probably will fail. I think that is what appeals to many investors about biotechnology. It is the treasure hunt of finding the next big breakthrough company. I will cover the companies. I will talk about the management, the financials, the science, the quality of the data, and propose my best guess at valuation. This section will be the most opinionated part of the website. This will be my thoughts and impressions based on my experience in investing in these companies. I will also disclose that I will personally be invested in many of these companies. You should always do your own research into any company and consult a professional financial advisor before making any investment.

The Science

The next section of the site will be about the science behind the companies. I tend to focus on a few key science themes with Genetics, Hematology, Immunology and Oncology. They tend to be very complementary with each other when it comes to diseases. I tend to focus on the use of technology in drug discovery, genetic diseases and oncology. I find having a focus on a few key topics offers expertise and helps me improve my success rates. I love teaching others about science. This section will include everything educational about topics like Hematology, Immunology, Genetics and Oncology. This is a learning resource. It is not necessary to know the science to be a biotechnology investor, but it really helps. I find that scientists, doctors, students and patients tend to like this section the most. Just a word of caution as this is not designed to be medical advice. You should always refer to your doctor.

Clinical Data

The last section will be about breaking down the clinical data. I think the hardest part of being a biotechnology investor is knowing how to read the clinical data, and how to figure out what it all means. There are a lot of technical endpoints that can confuse investors. Sometimes companies fail to design a good clinical trial. This happens too often. Many of these young companies are not experts in designing or running clinical trials. This section will be some teaching about the clinical trials and the data to look for, but a lot of it will be going through actual clinical data readouts by companies and breaking it down. I am not an expert in reading clinical data, but I have been doing it for many years. Most of the takes I give on clinical data will be just my point of view. Other investors might not agree with my point of view on any data readouts. That is OK. There is a lot to consider when looking at data to include data from competitors.
